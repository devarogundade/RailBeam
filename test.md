# Railbeam — the post‑payment era

Railbeam is a new way to **accept payments** and a new way to **sell**: instead of building a traditional ecommerce storefront, you deploy an **agentic sales attendant** that can chat, qualify, and close—then take payment seamlessly.

This is the **new era for payments**:
- Not “checkout pages” and abandoned carts.
- Not “one rigid integration” per payment method.
- **A programmable payment layer** where agents can transact safely, instantly, and globally.

## What Railbeam enables

- **Accept payments with x402**: native payment primitives designed for modern web flows—ideal for paywalls, API monetization, and agent-to-agent commerce.
- **Agentic chat sales agent**: an AI sales person that answers questions, handles objections, and guides the buyer to purchase—more like a human attendant than a static store.
- **One‑time transactions**: simple “buy now” payments for single items, services, or access.
- **Recurrent transactions**: subscriptions and ongoing billing for memberships, SaaS, retainers, and usage-based relationships.
- **Split payments**: automatically route funds to multiple parties (partners, affiliates, creators, teams) with transparent on-chain logic.
- **Send & receive by username + QR code**: consumer-friendly transfers without copy/pasting long addresses; share a QR for instant pay.
- **Credit card + fund direct from wallet**: support card-like UX and wallet-native rails so users can pay how they prefer.

## Storage, compute, and trust built in

- **0G Storage**: store and retrieve app assets and payment-related metadata with decentralized storage primitives.
- **0G Compute**: run compute workloads that power agent experiences and payment automation.
- **Encryption**: protect sensitive data and messages end-to-end so payments and commerce remain private by default.

## Agents as first-class payment actors

Railbeam supports an **EIP-based Agent ERC** concept: a standard way to represent agents as on-chain identities that can hold permissions, interact with contracts, and execute payment flows safely.

This unlocks “agent commerce” patterns like:
- An agent that can quote, invoice, and collect.
- An agent that can split settlement automatically.
- An agent that can manage renewals and receipts.

## Payment TypeScript SDK

Railbeam includes a **TypeScript payment SDK** so developers can integrate quickly:
- Create one-time or recurring payment flows.
- Trigger splits and settlements.
- Connect x402 resources to app actions.
- Power chat-to-pay experiences in web apps.

## Why it matters

### From storefronts to attendants
Instead of building a Shopify-like ecommerce site, Railbeam lets you create a **sales agent**: conversational, adaptive, and always-on.

### From “checkout” to “conversation”
Railbeam is built for a world where buyers prefer to ask questions, negotiate, and decide in context—then pay instantly without being thrown into a separate funnel.

## Example flows (landing-page friendly)

- **Chat → Quote → Pay**: a customer chats with your agent, gets a quote, pays once, and receives access immediately.
- **Chat → Subscribe**: the agent sells a plan, starts a recurring transaction, and manages renewals.
- **QR at point-of-sale**: scan a QR, pay by username, split settlement behind the scenes.
- **Wallet-first or card-like**: users can fund directly from a wallet, or choose a card-style experience for familiarity.
